Root Cause

The vast majority of allergies are a result of the immune system's overreaction to substances that are typically harmless, known as allergens. The immune system mistakes these allergens as a threat to the body and produces antibodies, such as IgE, to fight against them. When these antibodies encounter the allergens, they trigger the release of histamine and other chemicals in the body, causing allergy symptoms such as itching, swelling, hives, and difficulty breathing.

Common allergens include pollen, dust mites, animal dander, certain foods, and insect venom. While the exact cause of allergies is not yet fully understood, research suggests that genetics, environmental factors, and a weakened immune system may contribute to their development.

Existing Solutions...

Existing solutions for allergies typically involve medications such as antihistamines and steroids, immunotherapy, and avoidance of allergens.

While these solutions can be effective for some individuals, they often come with limitations and drawbacks. Medications may have unwanted side effects and may not be effective for all types of allergies, while immunotherapy can be time-consuming and expensive and sometimes results aren't seen for months or even years at a time.

Avoidance of allergens may be challenging or even impossible in some situations. There is a need for more innovative and effective solutions that can address the root causes of allergies and improve outcomes for allergy sufferers.
